privacy policy.

last updated: april 24, 2026. riveting stuff.

who we are

we're thatswassupps. we make streetwear and a game called floppy crops. this policy covers the game (web and ios), our website at thatswassupps.com, and the storefront.

what we collect — web version

almost nothing. seriously.

when you play floppy crops in a browser and submit a score to the leaderboard, you can enter a display name. that's optional. if you skip it, we call you "anon." we store that display name and your score. that's it.

what we collect — ios version

still almost nothing. ios just has its own plumbing, so we're laying it out.

game center. when you launch the app, ios asks if you want to sign into game center. if you say yes, apple gives us a "team player id" — a pseudonymous identifier that's unique to our app. we never see your real apple id, your name, or your email. when you finish a run, we submit your score to two game center leaderboards (alltime + weekly) so you can see where you rank. game center is run by apple and is governed by apple's privacy policy.

push notifications. after your first win, the app asks if you want notifications. if you say yes, apple gives us a "device token" so we can send the occasional ping (new event, leaderboard reset, that kind of thing). the token doesn't identify you — uninstall the app and it's gone. we send the token to our backend at thatswassupps.com/api/floppy-crops/push-token so the app can talk to your device.

crash reports. if you've opted in under ios settings → privacy & security → analytics & improvements → share with app developers, apple sends us anonymized crash logs through app store connect. that's apple's data flow, not ours; we read the logs to fix bugs.

what we don't collect

no email addresses. no phone numbers. no location data. no advertising identifier (idfa). no cookies. no tracking pixels. no analytics sdks. no ads. no selling your data. none of that.

where the data lives

web leaderboard data (display names and scores) is stored on supabase, a hosted database service. it's publicly visible on the leaderboard — that's the whole point.

ios leaderboard data lives with apple in game center, not on our servers.

push device tokens are stored in our backend so we can send notifications. they're not shared with anyone.

third party services

the web version sends high score notifications to our discord server via webhook. no personal data beyond the display name you chose.

the ios version uses apple game center and apple push notification service. both are apple. that's it — no other sdks.

children

the game is rated 4+ and we don't knowingly collect personal information from children. the optional display name in the web version is a freely chosen alias and is not personal information. the ios version doesn't collect anything that identifies a child.

your rights

want your scores removed from the web leaderboard? want your push token deleted? want any of this gone? email us at thatswassuppsofficial@gmail.com and we'll handle it.

game center scores are managed by apple — you can clear them yourself in game center settings or by deleting the app and reinstalling.

retention

we keep what we collect until you ask us to remove it, or until it's no longer useful — whichever comes first. push tokens are dropped automatically when apple tells us they've expired.

changes

if we ever change this policy, we'll update this page and bump the date at the top. but honestly, we're probably never going to collect more data. that sounds like work.
